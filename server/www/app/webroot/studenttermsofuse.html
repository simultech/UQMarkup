Participant Information for Students

Project Title

UQMarkUP: Effective feedback and consistent assessment for large cohorts with e-learning enabled 'in situ' commenting

Chief Investigators

Dr Kirsten Zimbardi, Dr Kay Colthorpe, Prof Phil Long, Dr Craig Engstrom, Dr Andrea Bugarcic

What this project is about

The Centre for Educational Innovation and Technology (CEIT) in partnership with Human Movement Studies (HMS) developed and trialled an e-learning system (UQMarkUP) which allows markers to provide students with detailed, specifically targeted and personalised feedback in a timely and efficient fashion. It allows markers to insert audio, handwritten and typed comments anywhere within the student’s work.

We are now extending the use of UQMarkUP to several large School of Biomedical Science courses, across a range of assessment tasks. In addition, a new feature has been developed to moderate tutor marking of student work.

The aims of this study are to advance, implement and evaluate an innovative audio-visual technology to optimise the quality, accessibility and efficacy of assessment feedback in a range of tertiary education settings.

The data we are collecting

To do this we need your help as a student studying a course through the School of Biomedical Science at the University of Queensland.

We will be collecting:

  • Your engagement with the feedback provided via UQMarkUP and traditional or existing methods of providing feedback
  • Your views on the method, mode and format of feedback provided to you

If you do agree to participate, data from university records may also be accessed. This will include information regarding:

  • The program you are enrolled in
  • Your academic performance

Importantly, participation in this study simply requires you to provide permission for your data from your assessment and university records to be accessed by the researchers.

You will also be asked to complete a 15 minute survey and may be invited to participate in a 30min-1hr interview to obtain your views about the feedback provided through the UQMarkUP system.

How we use this data & confidentiality

All information collected from you will be de-identified and/or allocated a code to enable the alignment of all data collected.

All information that is obtained will be strictly confidential and will only be accessed by the researchers. Taking part in this study is voluntary and you are free to withdraw from participating at any time. Feedback regarding the major findings of the research will be emailed to you on completion of the project.

We will provide you with information once the study has concluded

At the conclusion of the study, the major findings of the research will be compiled in “everyday” language and uploaded to the course Blackboard site. An announcement that this information is available will be emailed to you.

Risk to you & Benefits to you

There are no risks to you participating in this study beyond those that exist in normal daily life.

You will not receive any benefits from participating in this study other than possibly low value gifts for participating in interviews.

You may elect to withdraw at any time

The decision to participate in this study is entirely voluntary and you are free to withdraw your participation in this study at any time, without prejudice or repercussion. To do this, simply email the Project Leader – Dr Kirsten Zimbardi (k.zimbardi@uq.edu.au) stating your name, course details, and request to withdraw. The materials containing the data you have provided will be deleted from our records.

Informed Consent for Students

I have read the information letter about the research, and give my consent to participate in the study based on the understanding that:

  1. I am aware of the general purpose, methods and demands of the study
  2. The researchers are asking to use data about the ways in which I interact with my assessment feedback as part of this research, and will ask me to complete a short survey
  3. I may be contacted to participate in an interview about my experiences in using UQMarkUP and that this interview will be audio recorded, transcribed and the information used in this study
  4. I give permission for the researchers to access my assessment and university records
  5. All information I provide, or which is accessed from university records, will be kept confidential and will not be identifiable
  6. My participation in this study is voluntary and I am free to withdraw from the study or refuse to take part at any time, without negative consequences
